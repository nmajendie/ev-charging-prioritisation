# EV Charging Pilot Market Prioritisation — Strategy Memo

**Author:** Nick Majendie  
**Date:** March 2026  

---

## Key takeaway

Dense urban local authorities with relatively high EV adoption — particularly several London boroughs — represent the strongest near-term opportunities for EV charging pilots due to high likelihood of utilisation and scalable deployment insights.

---

## Recommendation

Prioritise initial pilot deployments in a small set of high-density authorities with strong EV penetration, including:

* Kensington and Chelsea
* Westminster
* Barnet
* Kingston upon Thames

These markets combine constrained off-street parking with growing EV ownership, increasing reliance on public charging infrastructure.

A second tier of high-density authorities with moderate EV adoption — such as Camden and Hammersmith & Fulham — should be considered for near-term expansion as demand accelerates.

---

## Why this matters

For early-stage charging networks, utilisation risk is a primary determinant of commercial success. Areas with both high EV ownership and dense urban form are more likely to generate consistent usage and provide operational learning that can inform broader rollout.

Focusing pilots in structurally constrained urban environments reduces demand uncertainty and improves capital efficiency.

---

## Approach

Local authorities were assessed using a simple decision framework combining:

* **EV adoption intensity** (EVs per 1,000 residents) to measure relative demand
* **Population density** (people per km²) as a proxy for limited private parking and reliance on public charging

These metrics were aggregated to Upper Tier Local Authority level using publicly available data. A composite score was created using percentile rankings, with sensitivity testing applying greater weight to density to reflect utilisation risk.

---

## Key insights

* Population density is a strong differentiator when identifying high-utilisation markets.
* London boroughs consistently rank highest due to urban form and adoption levels.
* Some suburban areas with high EV ownership but lower density may present lower utilisation potential and require more selective deployment.

---

## Risks and limitations

* Population density is an imperfect proxy for parking availability.
* EV registrations do not directly capture charging behaviour.
* Results should be interpreted as a comparative prioritisation rather than precise demand forecasts.

---

## Next steps

* Conduct site-level feasibility analysis within priority authorities
* Incorporate housing or parking data to refine demand estimates
* Validate assumptions through pilot deployments
* Expand framework to include commercial indicators such as income or traffic flows

---

**Bottom line:** A focused pilot strategy targeting dense urban authorities provides the strongest path to early utilisation and scalable learning for EV charging deployment.
